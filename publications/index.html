Books

AGP

Albert Power RHA 1881–1945

Judith Hill

Irish Academic Press · 2012 · ISBN 978-0-7165-3152-4

The definitive scholarly monograph on Power's life and work, by art historian Judith Hill. Drawing on family archives, museum collections, and original research, this is the most comprehensive account of Power's career yet published. Hill traces his training under John Hughes and Edouard Lantéri, his emergence as a central figure in Irish sculptural life, his extraordinary sequence of revolutionary death masks, his ecclesiastical commissions, and his public monuments.

The book contains a full catalogue of known works, extensive illustration from museum and family collections, and a scholarly reassessment of Power's place in the history of Irish art. Essential reading for anyone interested in his work or in the sculptural culture of early twentieth-century Ireland.

EoN

Expressions of Nationhood in Bronze, Stone and Glass

Síghle Bhreathnach-Lynch (ed.) & contributors

Edited collection

A scholarly collection addressing Irish sculpture and its role in the visual construction of national identity. Power's work — particularly his portrait sculpture and early career pieces — features in the discussion of how Irish artists used native materials and subjects to articulate a distinctively Irish artistic identity in the early twentieth century.

Exhibition Catalogues

RHA

Royal Hibernian Academy Annual Exhibition Catalogues

Royal Hibernian Academy, Dublin

Annual · 1906–1945

Power exhibited at the RHA from 1906 until his death in 1945, and the annual exhibition catalogues provide a year-by-year record of the works he submitted for public exhibition. These catalogues — held in the RHA archive and in the National Library of Ireland — are an essential primary source for reconstructing his output and the reception of his work during his lifetime.

NMI

The Death Mask in Irish History

National Museum of Ireland

Exhibition material · Collins Barracks, Dublin

The National Museum of Ireland's collection at Collins Barracks holds Power's death masks of Michael Collins and Arthur Griffith, along with related exhibition material contextualising their significance within Irish revolutionary history. The museum's catalogue records provide the authoritative provenance documentation for these works.
