Church & Religious Works

Devotional sculpture across Ireland

Alongside his revolutionary portraits and public memorials, Albert G. Power produced a substantial body of ecclesiastical and devotional work for churches and religious institutions across Ireland. These commissions — often in Portland stone or bronze — represent a quieter, more contemplative dimension of his practice, and many remain in place in churches that are open to the public today.

This page documents confirmed commissions outside Dublin. Power also produced significant religious works within the city, including at the Pro-Cathedral. Further entries will be added as research confirms additional locations.

Confirmed Locations

1935

Liam Lynch Monument

Sandstone round tower with four bronze wolfhounds · Newcastle, Clonmel, Co. Tipperary

A major public commission honouring General Liam Lynch (1893–1923), Chief of Staff of the Anti-Treaty IRA, who was mortally wounded on the Knockmealdown Mountains on 10 April 1923. The monument takes the form of a round tower over eighteen metres high on the mountainside, with four bronze wolfhounds at its base. Designed by engineer Denis Doyle from Clonmel and built by locals, it was unveiled on Sunday 31 March 1935 before an estimated crowd of 10,000 people from across Ireland. Power sculpted the four wolfhounds in his Berkeley Road yard.

1935

Cathal Brugha Plaque

Bronze relief plaque · Hammam Buildings, O'Connell Street Upper, Dublin

A bronze relief portrait of Cathal Brugha (1874–1922), placed on the facade of the Hammam Buildings on O'Connell Street — the site where Brugha was mortally wounded in July 1922. He was shot emerging from the building during the opening days of the Civil War and died the following day. The plaque was commissioned when the national health insurance headquarters was renamed in his honour.

1933

Portrait of Dr Bethel Solomons

Bronze · Rotunda Hospital, Parnell Square, Dublin

Portrait bust of Dr Bethel Solomons (1885–1965), Master of the Rotunda Maternity Hospital from 1925, celebrated gynaecologist, former Ireland rugby international, and friend of Arthur Griffith who had supported the Easter Rising. Solomons himself commented it was a fine likeness. It was shown at the RHA exhibition in 1934.

1937

Statue of Bishop Laurence Gillooly

Limestone · Gillooly Hall (formerly Community Hall), Temple Street, Sligo

Full-length figure of Bishop Laurence Gillooly (1858–1895), Bishop of Elphin, carved in limestone and placed on the front elevation of the Community Hall (formerly the Temperance Hall) opposite St Mary's Cathedral, Sligo. The hall had been erected in Gillooly's memory after his death. Completed April 1937, unveiled May 1937 by Dr Edward Dooly, the resident Bishop of Elphin. Power worked from photographs and oil paintings. The crozier was carved from a replica owned by the deceased.

1945

Statue of the Sacred Heart

Stone · Sacred Heart Church, Carndonagh, Co. Donegal

Power's last known commission. A full-length statue of the Sacred Heart, photographed in progress in the Berkeley Road yard in 1945 and reported in The Standard newspaper. The work was completed in the final year of Power's life — he died in July 1945.

c.1936

Madonna and Child

All Hallows College, Grace Park Road, Drumcondra, Dublin

Power displayed photographs of the Madonna and Child at All Hallows at his 1936 talk to the Academy of Christian Art, alongside his secular busts and public monuments, as a key example of his religious work. Date of original commission unconfirmed.
