In 1920, Albert Power worked in collaboration with the artist **Jack Morrow** on an altar and reredos for St Catherine's Church, Meath Street, Dublin — one of relatively few documented instances of Power partnering directly with another named artist on a single ecclesiastical commission, rather than executing the entire scheme independently.

St Catherine's, on Meath Street in Dublin's Liberties, has its own significant place in Irish history — the original St Catherine's Church on Thomas Street was where Robert Emmet was executed in 1803. The present church on Meath Street is a separate, later building. Power's 1920 contribution there came during the same year as his work on the Madonna and Christ Child group at All Hallows College and shortly before his admission to Brixton Prison to model Terence MacSwiney's life mask in October of that year.
