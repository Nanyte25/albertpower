Among Power's last major commissions, the three granite figures of Ireland's patron saints — St Patrick, St Brigid, and St Colmcille — were carved between 1943 and 1945 for the dome of the Sacred Heart Church in Carndonagh, Co. Donegal. Power died in 1945, and this commission represents some of his final work.

Carndonagh, on the Inishowen Peninsula, is one of the oldest Christian sites in Ireland, associated with St Patrick's mission to the north. The placement of the three saints on the church dome — exposed to the Atlantic climate of Donegal — required granite rather than the limestone or marble Power used for interior work. The three figures stand on the corners of the dome, weathered into the Donegal skyline.

The commission demonstrates the geographical reach of Power's reputation across Ireland in the final years of his life, and his sustained output even as his health declined.
