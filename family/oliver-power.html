The Third Generation

Oliver Power was the son of James Power and grandson of Albert George Power RHA. He is Mark Freer's grandfather — the direct link between the great Albert Power and the family member who created this archive.

Oliver carried the family craft into the third generation, working as a stonemason and sculptor and maintaining the Power tradition of working directly in stone rather than purely through casting. His speciality was carved lettering and stonework — a discipline that requires extraordinary precision and patience, and one in which the Power family had accumulated generations of knowledge.

The Robert Emmet Bridge Lettering

One of Oliver Power's most significant documented contributions is the carved lettering on the Robert Emmet Bridge on Harold's Cross, Dublin (1938). The bridge memorial, which commemorates the republican leader Robert Emmet, involved both Albert Power and Oliver in its execution — Albert designing and overseeing the sculptural elements, Oliver responsible for the precision stone lettering that gives the memorial its permanent inscribed character.

Carved lettering of this kind — cut directly into stone, permanent and unforgiving — was among the most technically demanding work a stonemason could undertake. There is no correction possible; every cut is final. That Oliver Power's lettering on the Emmet Bridge has endured for nearly a century without deterioration is a testament to his mastery of the craft.

The Family Studio

The Power family operated a stone yard and studio in Dublin that was central to their practice across three generations. The yard — documented in the family archive through photographs from as early as 1912 — was both a working space and a repository of the family's tools, methods, and knowledge.

Oliver grew up in and around this studio, learning the craft directly from his father James as James had learned from Albert. The continuity of knowledge across these three generations — from Albert's training under Lantéri in London at the turn of the century to Oliver's work in the latter half of the twentieth century — represents one of the most sustained traditions in Irish sculptural history.

A Personal Note

This archive was created by Mark Freer, Oliver Power's grandson and Albert G. Power's great-grandson. Oliver Power is Mark Freer's grandfather and the direct connection to the Power sculptural dynasty — the grandfather through whom the family's craft, its stories, and its archive passed to the next generation.

The photographs in this archive of Oliver at work, of the family stone yard, and of the Robert Emmet lettering were preserved through the family and form part of the living memory that this site exists to honour.

Robert Emmet Bridge — Carved Lettering

The Emmet Bridge Inscription

In 1938, Albert Power completed a bronze relief plaque of Robert Emmet (1778–1803) for the newly renamed Emmet Bridge at Clanbrassil Street Upper, Harold's Cross, Dublin — commissioned by the Fourth Battalion of the Dublin Brigade, Old IRA. The bronze was signed and dated in Irish: De Paor, 1938.

The limestone inscription on the plaque was carved by Oliver Power — a direct collaboration between father and son on one of Albert's final public commissions. The bridge can still be visited today at the Grand Canal, Harold's Cross Road, Dublin.
